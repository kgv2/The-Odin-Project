Al Pastor Tacos

Description

Al pastor tacos are a popular Mexican street food known for their flavorful marinated pork, which is traditionally cooked on a vertical spit.
The meat is thinly sliced and served on warm corn tortillas, often topped with onions, cilantro, and juicy pineapple.

This dish combines savory, sweet, and spicy flavors, making it a favorite among taco enthusiasts.
Al pastor tacos are a delicious representation of Mexico's rich culinary heritage and vibrant food culture.

Ingredients

Serving Size: 24 Tacos for 4

  • 2 lbs pork shoulder, thinly sliced
  • 3 dried guajillo chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 cup pineapple juice
  • 1/4 cup white vinegar
  • 1/2 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 chipotle pepper in adobo sauce
  • 2 tbsp achiote paste
  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground cloves
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
  • 1 cup fresh pineapple, diced
  • Corn tortillas
  • Chopped onion, for garnish
  • Chopped cilantro, for garnish
  • Lime wedges, for serving

Steps

  1. Soak the dried guajillo and ancho chiles in hot water until softened, then blend with garlic, pineapple juice, vinegar, onion, chipotle, achiote paste, oregano, cumin, cloves, salt, and pepper to make a marinade.
  2. Marinate the sliced pork shoulder in the blended sauce for at least 4 hours or overnight in the refrigerator.
  3. Grill or cook the marinated pork over medium-high heat until cooked through and slightly charred.
  4. Warm the corn tortillas and fill them with the cooked pork.
  5. Top with diced pineapple, chopped onion, cilantro, and serve with lime wedges.
